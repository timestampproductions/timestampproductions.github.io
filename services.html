SENIOR CITIZENS AND ACTIVE RETIREMENT

  • An Síol's Meals Service provides a local Meals On Wheels delivery service along with a Sit-Down Dinner Service Monday–Friday in Aughrim Court and St Bricins Park.
  • Our Minibus Service is used to transport older people to Meals Service, Outings, Day Trips, Pension Collection and to activities within the community.
  • Grants for pendant alarms can be accessed through An Síol CDP for peace of mind and security in the home.
  • Activities Service provides classes in arts & crafts, and mobility exercise. We are continually changing and developing our activities. The activities take place in Aughrim Court, St Bricins Park and Drumalee.

CHILD AND YOUTH EDUCATION SERVICES

    Supporting the education of young people is a priority for An Síol CDP.

    We offer the following services:

  • Afterschool clubs for Primary school students four afternoons a week. This service provides a positive, fun space and plenty of support with doing homework.
  • Family learning club where junior and senior infants with a parent take part in fun learning activities. • One-to-one tuition providing extra support to students studying for the junior and leaving certificate.
  • One-to-one tuition for secondary students who are worried about falling behind.
  • Provide volunteering opportunities through our tuition programme.

All educational services provided by An Siol are free of charge.

An Síol CDP links with other educational groups and organisations locally. We are committed to listening to what you have to say about the educational needs of young people in the community.

For further information on the Child and Youth Education Services, please call 01 677 5741 or drop into the office at An Síol CDP, 19 Manor St, Dublin 7.

WELFARE RIGHTS INFORMATION

An Síol provides a free confidential Welfare Rights Information Service. We provide information and assistance with issues such as:

  • Jobseeker’s Payments
  • Social Welfare Payments
  • Back to Work options
  • Taxation and Welfare to Work
  • Education and Training Options
  • Secondary Benefits – including Rent Supplement, Medical Card, etc.
  • General Welfare Issues
  • Support, advocacy and referrals for clients who are having difficulties in areas such as social welfare, housing, family violence, social assistance, employment, debt management etc. is also provided

In addition we also provide information, support and representation to people making Social Welfare Appeals. Our service is totally free, confidential and impartial.

WORK WITH LOCAL RESIDENTS

A key part of the work of An Síol is supporting local residents and tenant groups.

We are available to:

  • Meet with groups.
  • Hear about issues going on for you in your local area.
  • Assist with organising meetings, writing letters, telephone calls, photocopying – or in any way that helps residents who are working to improve their own local area.
  • Help groups to develop their own skills in running meetings, committee skills, applying for funding.
  • Help advertise services, facilities, events happening locally.

If you feel any of these services would be of use to you, please call 01 677 5741 or drop into the office at An Síol CDP, 19 Manor Street, Dublin 7.
